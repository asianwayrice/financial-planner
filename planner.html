The 50/30/20 Rule

A popular US budgeting framework to organize your take-home pay:

50% Needs: Rent, groceries, utilities, and insurance.
30% Wants: Dining out, hobbies, and subscriptions.
20% Financial: Debt payments and emergency savings.

Building Your Safety Net

Financial experts in the US generally recommend two types of savings:

  • Emergency Fund: 3–6 months of essential expenses kept in a High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA).
  • Compound Interest: The earlier you save, the more your interest earns interest.

Debt Payoff Strategies

The Avalanche Method

Pay the highest interest rate first. This saves you the most money in total interest.

The Snowball Method

Pay the smallest balance first. This creates psychological "wins" to keep you motivated.

Understanding Your Paycheck

In the US, your "Gross Pay" is not what you keep. Here’s what disappears:

FICA Taxes: 7.65% for Social Security and Medicare.
401(k) Match: If your employer matches contributions, it's essentially a 100% return on your money.
Income Tax: Federal and State brackets vary based on where you live.
